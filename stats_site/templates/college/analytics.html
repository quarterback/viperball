METRIC GLOSSARY

WAR — Wins Above Replacement

Borrowed from baseball. Measures a player's total value in wins compared to a replacement-level player at the same position.

Formula: WAR = (YAR × 0.08 pts/yd) ÷ 6 pts/win
YAR = (Y/T − replacement level) × volume factor × touches
Volume factor uses log scaling to prevent high-volume domination.

Replacement levels: ZB 3.8, Viper 4.2, HB 3.5, WB 3.6, SB 3.4, Keeper 2.0

ZBR — Zeroback Rating

The Viperball equivalent of NFL passer rating, built for the Zeroback position. Uses the classic 0–158.3 scale.

Four components (each 0–2.375, like NFL passer rating):
a) Yards Per Touch (efficiency)
b) Touchdown Rate (scoring)
c) Fumble Rate inverted (ball security)
d) Lateral Accuracy (completion%)

Scale: 158.3 = perfect · ~100 = great · ~85 = good · ~65 = average · <40 = poor

VPR — Viper Rating

Position-specific composite for the Viper wild-card role. Same 0–158.3 scale as ZBR for direct comparison, but emphasizes explosiveness and all-purpose production.

Four components (each 0–2.375):
a) Yards Per Touch (lower threshold than ZBR — Vipers should be electric)
b) Touchdown Rate
c) Fumble Rate inverted
d) All-Purpose Efficiency (total yards per touch across all phases)

Key difference from ZBR: Uses all-purpose yards instead of lateral accuracy.

WPA — Win Probability Added

Measures how much each player's involvement shifted the team's probability of winning. Computed from Expected Points (EP) changes on each play, attributed to participants.

Formula: WPA = EP(after play) − EP(before play)
EP is calculated from field position + down via lookup table.
Distributed among players involved in each play.

WPA/P (per play) normalizes for volume — shows efficiency independent of usage.
